--- layout: single classes: wide --- Faster, higher, stronger is the Olympic game slogan, so is the expectation for legged robot motion generation. Executing Dynamic and swift motion as human beings, e.g., hammering a nail, swiftly grabbing a box, or jumping, leads to issues that are not fully addressed yet by the state-of-the-art robot control and hardware design. Most of the robots are not manufactured to mitigate high external impulses. Thus fragile components, such as the harmonics, significantly limit the maximum impulse a robot can take. Unlike robots mounted on a fixed-base, under-actuated robots have to balance themselves using contacts that can only provide unilateral supporting forces. Even worse, the contact areas are sometimes limited, e.g., a partial foothold, and change over time. Thus, to accelerate the robot performance, we would like to bring the latest developments include but not limited to:
  • How to perform timely impact/collision detection?
  • How to improve the hardware design to mitigate high external impulses?
  • How to properly model the frictional impact dynamics ?
  • How to balance the under-actuated floating-base while executing high dynamic motion?
  • How to stabilize the robot states given multiple impacts?
  • How to preview and exploit the impact-induced state jumps for model-based controllers?
