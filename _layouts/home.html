--- layout: single classes: wide --- Faster, higher, stronger is the Olympic game slogan, so is the robot motion generation. Executing Dynamic and swift motion as human beings, e.g., hammering a nail or jumping, leads to step changes of the robot states that are not fully addressed by the state-of-the-art robot hardware and control design. Most of the robots are not manufactured to mitigate high external impulses. Thus fragile components, such as the harmonics, significantly limit the maximum impulse a robot can take. Unlike robots mounted on a fixed-base, under-actuated robots have to balance themselves using contacts that can only provide unilateral supporting forces. Even worse, the contact areas are sometimes limited, e.g., a partial foothold, and change over time. The high-dynamic-motion-induced state jumps, of either the links or the center of mass, break the continuity assumptions of the classical force control laws, desire proper frictional impact dynamics model to preview the sudden state jump and challenge the state-of-the-art balancing controllers. Thus to accelerate the robot motion, we would like to bring the latest developments include but not limited to:
  • How to perform timely impact/collision detection?
  • How to improve the hardware design to mitigate high external impulses?
  • How to properly model the frictional impact dynamics ?
  • How to balance the under-actuated floating-base while executing high dynamic motion?
  • How to stabilize the robot states given multiple impacts?
  • How to preview the for model-based controllers?
